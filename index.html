who is this guy?

I'm an senior at Yale, studying Molecular Biophysics & Biochemistry with a concentration in Chemical Biology, and a certificate in Medieval Studies. I'm interested in how our immune system uses complex (and sometimes counterintuitive) biochemical signals to manage the insanely difficult task of keeping us alive.

Outside science, I think a lot about the Middle Ages; I'm especially interested in questions of how Medieval conceptions of self and other influence how we think today! I'm also very involved with comedy at Yale. I regularly perform stand-up shows and was previously the Online Editor in Chief of The Yale Record, the world's oldest English-languge comedy magazine. I was also Co-President of the Yale Nepali Students Society and had the privilege of supporting our community on campus during a recent period of great uncertainty in Nepal.

Research

Carrie Lucas Lab, Yale University May 2024 — present

In the Lucas Lab, I study how CD8+ T cells sustain immunological memory, using ELF4-deficiency, an extremely rare inborn error of immunity, as a lens onto immunological signaling. I use several molecular, cellular, and computational tools/techniques for this project and I have been supported by multiple fellowships.

Robin Berlin Fellowship May 2025 — Dec 2025

Awarded to study both immunology and the history of the Black Death. This culminated in a paper re: antisemitism for Prof. Ivan Marcus' class "Medieval Jews, Christians, and Muslims In Conversation." This paper was my first foray into historiography. The full essay has an obnoxiously long title and you can read it here; a "Cliffnotes" version presentation I gave the class is linked here!

Other Intellectual Adventures

Screenwriter for the Yale Symphony Orchestra's 2025 Halloween Show Mar 2025 — Oct 2025

My friends Diana Yang, Ray Nassar, and I wrote the screenplay for the most-attended student performance of the year (>2,500 ticketed attendees + some others who managed to sneak in). The Halloween Show is a silent film set to a soundtrack performed by the Yale Symphony Orchestra. We had a lot of fun doing this; getting to write lines for Sir Paul McCartney was especially cool. I'm not allowed to distribute the movie but you can read the screenplay: Lord of Yale: Fellowship of the GPT! Many thanks to Mina Chang, Juliet Mandelbrot, and Ben Graham for bringing our vision to life.

A Very Close Reading of Dante's Comedy Sep 2024 — April 2025

I am a big fan of the 13th century Italian poet Dante Alighieri. I came to love his work through Prof. Heather Webb's course, "Dante in Translation." The final essay I wrote for this class, titled "The Price of Paradise: Female Sexuality, Free Will, and Moral Agency in Dante's Comedy" won the 2024-2025 prize for the best essay in the Italian Studies department. The presentation I gave for a student symposium is linked here!

Investigating of the History of Eugenics at Yale Feb 2025

I spent a good portion of February 2025 thinking about the history of eugenics at Yale. I interviewed the students and professors who are working to make sense of our university's complex history surrounding race and race science. I compiled my findings into an article for The Yale Herald titled "Genetic Fragments: Synthesizing Yale’s History of Eugenics." This article is now used as instructional material for Professor Daniel Martinez HoSang's class "Eugenics and its Afterlives."
