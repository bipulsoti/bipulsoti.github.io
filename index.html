About Me

Hello! I'm a student at Yale University studying Molecular Biophysics and Biochemistry + Medieval Studies. I'm particularly interested in the biochemistry of the adaptive immune system. Currently, I work on interferon gamma signaling and how it influences the dynamics of CD8+ T cell activation and memory. I do this work at the laboratory of Prof. Carrie Lucas at the Yale School of Medicine. My interests are generally in the world of immune regulation and signaling. This includes the function of the AIRE protein and the crosstalk between Th1, Th2, and Th17 cells. In addition to my interests in classical immunology, I'm eager to explore the applications of organic chemistry in my work (we've recently started thinking about degrons at our lab, which is very exciting). Outside science, I keep myself busy through writing. When it comes to Medieval Studies, I have written about Dante's Comedy and the Black Death. These are topics I feel very strongly about and you can learn more about them below. Some of my other projects include the screenplay for the Yale Symphony Orchestra's 2025 Halloween Show, a lengthy article about the history of eugenics at Yale, and my work as the Online Editor in Chief of the Yale Record.
